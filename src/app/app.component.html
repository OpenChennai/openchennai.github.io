FAQ

What is Open Chennai?

Open Chennai is a collective effort by the citizens of Chennai to make our city better. We report and keep track of civic issues within the city.

What do we gain by reporting and tracking issues? Is any action guaranteed to fix the reported issues?

Our initial aim is to bring concerns of the citizens into the limelight and never lose track of them. When we report and track issues together, we reach a larger audience. The sum of our efforts becomes larger than its parts. This would be equivalent to something like Wikipedia, but for the city's civic issues. We hope to get involved in taking action as well, but no promises for now.

Where do I view already reported issues?

We are currently using GitHub to track issues. Follow the links to find issues under different sections.

  • Water and Sanitation
  • Electricity
  • Garbage
  • Public Transport
  • Traffic
  • Trees
  • Parks and Playgrounds

How do I report issues?

The easy way

Report issues to us on Twitter or on Facebook.

The better way

Raise GitHub issues on the respective repositories. If you are not familiar with raising GitHub issues, create a GitHub account and find out how to create issues here.

Are there general guidelines for reporting an issue?

Please be as detailed as possible and add photos, links to the geographical location of the issue, if possible.

Who is running this effort? What are your motivations?

We are just a group of individuals coming together to solve our own problems. We have no affiliations to any political entity and our intention is to build a community of like-minded citizens to solve our city's problems.
