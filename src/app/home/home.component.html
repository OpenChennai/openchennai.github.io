    FAQ

  • What do we gain by reporting and tracking issues?

    • We want to record concerns of citizens in an open manner and never lose track of them.

  • Is any action guaranteed to fix the reported issues?

    • We hope to get involved in taking action as well, but no promises for now.

  • If no action is guaranteed, what is the actual benefit?

    • Think of Wikipedia. Now imagine something like that for the city's civic issues. Documenting issues is the first step towards solving them.

  • Is there any benefit for me, individually?

    • We even have a leaderboard below, if you are into such things!

  • Where do I view already reported issues?

    • We are currently using GitHub to track issues. Follow the links to find issues under different sections.
    • Roads
    • Water and Sanitation
    • Electricity
    • Garbage
    • Public Transport
    • Traffic
    • Trees
    • Parks and Playgrounds

  • How do I report issues?

    • Choose 1 of 2 the ways:

    1. Reach out to us on social networks

      • Send a tweet to us on @OpenChennai
      • Message us on Facebook - Open Chennai
      • Mention @OpenChennai on Instagram

    2. Raise a GitHub issue directly

      3. Raise GitHub issues directly on the respective projects/repositories listed above. If you are not familiar with raising GitHub issues, create a GitHub account and then create issues here: https://github.com/OpenChennai.

  • Are there general guidelines for reporting an issue?

    • Use the following template at the beginning when creating a new issue:
    • Reporter: name of reporter
    • Link: link to their social account
    • Description: description of the issue
    Please be as detailed as you can and add photos, links to the geographical location of the issue, if possible.

  • Who is running this effort? What are your motivations?

    • We are just a group of individuals coming together to solve our own problems. We have no affiliations to any political entity and our intention is to build a community of like-minded citizens to solve our city's problems.
